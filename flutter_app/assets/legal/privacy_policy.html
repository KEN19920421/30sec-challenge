30sec Challenge ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application and related services (collectively, the "Service").

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By accessing or using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information You Provide

When you create an account or use the Service, we may collect the following information directly from you:

  • Account Information: Email address, username, display name, and password.
  • Profile Information: Profile photo and biography.
  • User Content: Videos you record and submit as part of challenges, comments, and other content you post.
  • Communications: Information you provide when contacting our support team.
  • Purchase Information: In-app purchase receipts and transaction history (payment processing is handled by Apple App Store or Google Play Store; we do not directly collect or store credit card numbers).

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you use the Service, certain information is collected automatically:

  • Device Information: Device type, operating system version, unique device identifiers, and device tokens for push notifications.
  • Usage Data: Pages viewed, features used, interactions with content (votes, gifts, follows), session duration, and app crash reports.
  • Log Data: IP address, access times, referring URLs, and browser or app version.

1.3 Information from Third-Party Services

If you sign in using a third-party authentication provider (such as Google Sign-In or Sign in with Apple), we may receive your name, email address, and profile picture as authorized by you through that provider.

3. Data Sharing and Third-Party Services

We do not sell your personal information. We share your data only with the following categories of service providers, solely to operate and improve the Service:

3.1 Firebase (Google LLC)

We use Firebase for analytics, crash reporting, and push notifications. Firebase may collect device identifiers, usage data, and crash logs. See Firebase Privacy Policy.

3.2 Google AdMob (Google LLC)

We use AdMob to serve advertisements, including rewarded ads. AdMob may collect device identifiers, IP addresses, and usage data to deliver personalized or non-personalized ads. See Google Privacy Policy.

3.3 Amazon Web Services (AWS S3)

User-uploaded videos and profile photos are stored on Amazon S3. AWS processes this data on our behalf. See AWS Privacy Policy.

3.4 SendGrid (Twilio Inc.)

We use SendGrid to deliver transactional emails such as password resets and account notifications. SendGrid processes your email address on our behalf. See Twilio Privacy Policy.

We may also disclose your information:

  • To comply with applicable laws, regulations, or legal processes.
  • To enforce our Terms of Service or protect the rights, privacy, safety, or property of us, our users, or others.
  • In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or a portion of our assets (you will be notified of any such change).

4. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Service. Specifically:

  • Account data: Retained until you delete your account.
  • User content (videos): Retained until you delete the content or your account, whichever comes first.
  • Usage and analytics data: Retained in aggregated or anonymized form for up to 24 months.
  • Purchase records: Retained as required by applicable tax and financial regulations (typically 7 years).

When you delete your account, we will delete or anonymize your personal information within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.

5. Your Rights and Choices

5.1 All Users

Regardless of your location, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you.
  • Delete your account and associated data through the in-app Settings.
  • Opt out of push notifications through your device settings.
  • Opt out of personalized advertising through your device's ad settings (e.g., "Limit Ad Tracking" on iOS, "Opt out of Ads Personalization" on Android).

5.2 European Economic Area (EEA) Residents — GDPR

If you are located in the EEA, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right of rectification: Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Right to restrict processing: Request we limit how we use your data.
  • Right to data portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: File a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

5.3 California Residents — CCPA / CPRA

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to know: Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information collected about you.
  • Right to delete: Request deletion of your personal information.
  • Right to opt out of sale: We do not sell your personal information. If this changes, we will provide a clear opt-out mechanism.
  • Right to non-discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at support@30secchallenge.app. We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law.

6. Children's Privacy

The Service is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at support@30secchallenge.app and we will promptly delete such information.

Users between the ages of 13 and 17 must have the consent of a parent or legal guardian to use the Service. In the European Economic Area, the minimum age may be higher in accordance with local law (up to 16 years old).

7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

As a mobile application, 30sec Challenge does not use browser cookies. However, we and our third-party partners may use the following technologies:

  • Device identifiers: Such as Advertising ID (IDFA/GAID) for analytics and advertising purposes.
  • Firebase Analytics: Collects app usage events and session data.
  • AdMob SDK: May use device information to serve relevant advertisements.

You can reset or limit ad tracking through your device settings at any time. On iOS, we will request your permission via the App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt before accessing your IDFA.

While we strive to protect your personal information, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.
